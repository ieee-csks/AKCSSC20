UI Challenge

UI Challenge

Rules

Five themes will be provided. Participants can choose a problem statement from them and should submit the final draft before 25th November 2020. Divided into two categories:-
  1. Girls only
  2. General

Guidelines

Master challenge in 5 steps

Problem Statement - Step 1
Go through the problem definitions to find out which one you can work wonders on. Select the best problem statement and start working on it.

Audience - Step 2
Learn more about an audience that will use your future solution. Who they are?
  1. List what are the categories of people you want to focus on.
  2. List what potential motivations and fears (concerns) your category might have.
  3. List potential situations (when and where) people face a problem you solving.
  4. List ideas through what digital products you can offer your solution to the audience.


Products - Step 3
“There is probably an app for that”. Look for the apps and services that already trying to solve this problem. Make a list:
  •  Products that already work with your audience
  •  How many users they have (estimations)
  •  What their missions are
  •  How they make money (business model)


Solutions - Step 4
Note ideas of features that can extend existing apps' missions and help to solve a problem. For each idea figure out:
  •  Feature description
  •  How users will benefit from it
  •  How a company might benefit from it

And then start prototyping.

Submission - Step 5
Using our guides prepare your submission and apply via AKCSSC website.

Themes:-
  1. AKCSSC Website Revamp
  2. Design an Audiobook or Podcasting mobile app that is highly personal, highly interactive, and with the ability to bring even more utility to the user than a book ever could.
  3. Music Player App.
  4. Design a file management system accessed and organised through Virtual Reality.
  5. Design an interface that gives homeowners the ability to get information about their house, via IoT devices, from a remote location.
